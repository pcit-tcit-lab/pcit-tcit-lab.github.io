Parent-Child Interaction Therapy

Parent-Child Interaction Therapy (PCIT) is an evidence-based treatment for children 2 to 7 years of age with social, emotional, and behavioral challenges. Over the past 4 decades, PCIT has documented success addressing a wide variety of emotional and behavioral difficulties, including:

  • Physical & Verbal Aggression
  • Destruction of Property
  • Noncompliance / Defiance
  • Hyperactivity / Inattention
  • Emotional Dysregulation
  • Difficulties with Attachment

TCIT-C was designed to improve the social, emotional, and behavioral competence for children 3 to 6 years of age. Additionally, TCIT-C has been shown to increase teacher-efficacy and job satisfaction for early childhood educators.

Similar to PCIT, TCIT-C is used to reduce challenging classroom behaviors, including:

  • Physical & Verbal Aggression
  • Destruction of Property
  • Noncompliance / Defiance
  • Hyperactivity / Inattention
  • Emotional Dysregulation
  • Negative Teacher-Child Relationships
